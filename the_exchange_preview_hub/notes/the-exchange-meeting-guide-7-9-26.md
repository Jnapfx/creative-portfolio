### E. How It Works

Use a simple step-by-step process.

#### 1. Employers Submit Opportunities

Businesses submit a project, internship, or short-term role through the intake form.

#### 2. Candidates Express Interest

Candidates submit their background, skills, interests, availability, resume, and relevant links.

#### 3. The Exchange Reviews for Fit

Stamford Partnership / SW CT Tech Hub staff review both employer submissions and candidate interest forms.

#### 4. Matching Begins

Aligned candidates are surfaced to employers from a curated pool. Candidate referrals are based on fit, readiness, and availability.

#### 5. Work-Based Learning Begins

The employer and candidate begin the internship, project, or short-term role with clear expectations, supervision, and deliverables.

#### 6. Outcomes Are Documented

The Exchange collects feedback from both sides and uses the learning to improve future cycles.

## 7. Backend / Administrative Workflow

The backend does not need to be complex for Phase 1. The scope supports a simple workflow that allows staff to review submissions, organize information, and coordinate matching manually.

### Recommended Phase 1 Backend Options

Ask Patricia where she wants submissions to live initially:

1. **Google Sheets**
   - Simple and familiar.
   - Easy to review and share internally.
   - Good for pilot testing.

2. **WordPress Form Database**
   - Keeps submissions inside WordPress.
   - Useful if they prefer everything in one place.
   - May require plugin setup and permissions.

3. **Email Notifications + Spreadsheet Backup**
   - Staff receive immediate alerts.
   - Spreadsheet stores all submissions.
   - Good balance for a simple pilot.

4. **Airtable**
   - More structured than Google Sheets.
   - Easier for filtering, tagging, and status tracking.
   - Better if they expect a growing number of submissions.

### Recommended Workflow

#### Employer Pathway

1. Employer submits opportunity.
2. Stamford Partnership staff review scope, fit, readiness, supervision, and timeline.
3. If not ready, staff follow up.
4. If ready, opportunity is added to the internal opportunity pool.
5. Opportunity may be shared with partners or posted publicly later, depending on approval.
6. Candidates are matched manually.

#### Candidate Pathway

1. Candidate expresses interest.
2. Staff review skills, background, readiness, availability, and alignment.
3. If candidate is not ready, staff follow up or refer to a cohort/program lead.
4. If ready, candidate is added to the validated pool.
5. Candidate is considered for matching when opportunities become available.

## 8. AI Recommendation for Phase 1

Do not push advanced AI too hard in this meeting. Keep the recommendation lightweight and aligned with the pilot.

Suggested talking point:

> “For the pilot phase, I recommend keeping AI lightweight. Instead of building a complex matching system right away, we can focus on administrative support such as organizing submissions, summarizing opportunity descriptions, identifying incomplete forms, and helping staff manage follow-up. This keeps the first launch practical and easier to maintain.”

### Appropriate AI Uses for Phase 1

- Categorize employer submissions by opportunity type.
- Summarize long opportunity descriptions.
- Identify incomplete or unclear form responses.
- Suggest follow-up questions for staff.
- Help organize candidate interests by skills or availability.

### Avoid in Phase 1 Unless Separately Scoped

- Automated candidate scoring.
- Live AI matching between candidates and employers.
- CRM integration.
- Applicant tracking system functionality.
- Private candidate dashboards.
- Employer login portals.
- External database or API integrations.
